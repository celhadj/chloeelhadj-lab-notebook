Human approval gate: every draft is posted to a private review channel before anything reaches a customer. Nothing sends automatically — a person approves each batch, and every run leaves a timestamped audit trail of what was found, filtered, and drafted.

Lessons Learned

  • Activity matters more than raw count. Filtering by order history focuses effort on customers actually worth reaching.
  • Reps can't update records directly. Emails now direct them to the right internal contact instead.
  • Internal and customer-facing content should differ. Order history belongs in internal notes, not customer emails.
  • Always generate a draft. Even accounts with no rep or email on file get a manual template, so nothing falls through the cracks.
  • Timezone-aware date handling avoids miscounting expirations near month boundaries.
  • Card-type names are pulled live from the platform rather than hardcoded, so the pipeline stays correct as card programs change.
