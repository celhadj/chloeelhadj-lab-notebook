Human approval gate: every draft is posted to a private review channel before anything reaches a customer. No customer communication is sent automatically — a person approves each batch, and every run leaves a timestamped audit trail of what was found, filtered, and drafted.

Lessons Learned

  • Not every record deserves equal attention. Activity history is a better prioritization signal than simply finding every expired card.
  • Technical workflows have organizational constraints. Emails now direct them to the right internal contact instead.
  • Contexts belong in the right place. Internal drafts can include operational history; customer communication should stay focused on the immediate request.
  • Handle exceptions deliberately. Accounts without an assigned representative still receive a prepared internal draft instead of disappearing from the workflow, so nothing falls through the cracks.
  • Timezone-aware date handling avoids miscounting expirations near month boundaries.
  • Card-type names are pulled live from the platform rather than hardcoded, so the pipeline stays correct as card programs change.
